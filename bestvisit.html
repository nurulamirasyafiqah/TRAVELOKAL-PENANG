Top 3 Must-Visit Hotspots

Experience the best Penang has to offer at these iconic locations!


Kek Lok Si Temple


Situated in Air Itam, Kek Lok Si is the largest Buddhist temple in Malaysia. Known as the "Temple of Supreme Bliss," it features a striking seven-story Pagoda of Rama VI and a towering statue of the Goddess of Mercy, Kuan Yin. The temple complex combines Chinese, Thai, and Burmese architectural styles and is a significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists.

View Location

Penang Hill


Standing at 833 meters above sea level, Penang Hill offers panoramic views of the island and mainland. Visitors can reach the summit via a funicular railway. At the top, attractions include The Habitat, featuring a rainforest canopy walk, and historical sites like the Bel Retiro bungalow.

View Location

Khoo Kongsi


Khoo Kongsi is one of the most impressive clan houses in Malaysia, reflecting the heritage of the Khoo clan. Situated in Cannon Square, it features intricate carvings and elaborate architecture, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Chinese community in Penang.
